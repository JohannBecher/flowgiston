The Abundant Exchange

AbundX wants to build you a personal online application so you can participate in exchanges of goods and services where:

  • You can give something to one person and get something in return from another
  • Your privacy is protected by you
  • You have access to a robust reputation system
  • Your communications are secure

Without:

  • Converting everything to a common value like cash or exchange currencies
  • The limits of trying to match the needs and offers between just two people
  • A central controller or moderator

AbundX

Welcome to AbundX where we are building the AbundX Agent that can be used to create efficient and trustworthy peer to peer exchanges: Abundant Exchanges.

The AbundX Agent is your personal online representative to help you participate in peer-to-peer exchanges while protecting your privacy and providing a robust reputation system.

The AbundX Agent will enable a multi-way exchange of goods and services without cash or a central authority.

In an Abundant Exchange, or AbundX, you can give something to one person and receive something you value as much or more from another person. The AbundX helps link a circle of people who each give something and receive something in return.

Each person controls an application we call an AbundX Agent, that links with other Agents. People can create an authenticated and secure connection to each other through their Agents. This connection is called peer to peer because no central controller or coordinator is necessary for two Agents to communicate. Every AbundX Agent is equal and no Agent has special powers over other Agents.

In addition to connecting one to one, Agents can connect to a group of Agents, we call a Graph. A message to the Graph can be accessed by any Agent in the Graph. An Abundant Exchange is a Graph of AbundX Agents who wish to exchange goods and services.

A message posted to an AbundX is like posting to a bulletin board. Anyone who has access to the bulletin board can post and read any message, even those created before she became a member.

Messages to an AbundX are offers and needs. AbundX Agents provide tools for sorting, filtering, and posting messages. An AbundX Agent can recognize when it is part of a closed loop of offers and needs. Each AbundX Agents in the circle participates in a distributed transaction that ensures that either everyone agrees to the exchange, or no one is obligated.

AbundX: A more detailed description

The technology we have developed facilitates a multi-way exchange of subjective value without conversion to a central currency or any requirement of a central authority or central exchange.

This multi-way exchange system more closely resembles pre-currency economies, but uses technology to be even more efficient, reliable, and secure than centralized currencies.

Mechanisms for establishing secure connections independent of personal identity, enabling variable levels of trust, and verification of information through a Web of Provenance are provided in an infrastructure that is more secure than standard Internet technologies.

Key behaviors of the infrastructure are based upon a mathematical model and can be proven with formal methods. For more information about the enabling peer-to-peer technology we call “Agents” please visit QualityofID.com.

We have already done all the heavy lifting on our own dime. Many person years in sweat equity and hundreds of thousands of dollars have gone into defining and building the supporting technology to date. The only remaining technology barriers that need to be addressed are user experience improvements and installation streamlining. Both are well understood using established skill sets.

Please see our Kickstarter campaign to help us make Abundant Exchanges possible or volunteer.

FAQ about the AbundX Exchange

How can each person receive something of more value than what he or she gave? Doesn’t someone have to lose?

Because we all value different qualities differently, it is possible for everyone to get back more than what they gave. For example, when I have plenty of something I may be willing to exchange it for something I need more. It is very possible, and perhaps typical, that everyone in a circle of exchanges may consider what they receive to be of greater value than that was given. In a centralized system, either currency or barter, someone probably looses, since the central authority usually takes some percentage of every transaction.

What are the tax implications of a AbundX Exchange?

We are not qualified to offer tax advice but can point out these characteristics that may apply:

  1. There is no central authority to monitor exchanges, so there is no entity that can be made responsible for reporting exchanges.
  2. Exchanges are not reduced to a dollar value, so assessing the dollar value of an exchange is open to wide interpretation.
  3. Exchanges are not limited to agreements between two people. There may be several people involved in a single transaction. What is given is independent of what is received. In other words: my obligation to deliver an offer is in effect even if the person delivering an offer to me reneges. My obligation to deliver is enforced by a desire to maintain my reputation, not enforced by a contract.
Aren’t Abundant Exchange just like Socialism?

Quite the opposite. Currency systems are much more “socialist” than a distributed exchange system because currency systems are centrally controlled and require everything exchanged to be reduced to a single measurement determined by the society. A distributed exchange system allows each person to determine the qualities of goods and service he or she most values.

Why do I need to access the exchange through an Agent and not just post offers and needs to a central location?

In addition to secure communications and privacy protections, using an Agent in an exchange allows everyone in the exchange to see a history of transactions. History builds reputation and confidence in the exchange allowing higher value goods and services to be exchanged and promises made (stored value) over longer time periods. Communication through Agents provides assurances of the accuracy of claims made through the Agent using the Web of Provenance.

How is the Abundant Exchange different from local and exchange currencies?

Local and exchange-based currencies can address the issue of scarcity, and to some extent the centralization bias through expiration and devaluation. Local currencies allow more complex exchanges than this early version of the AbundX Agent, but at the “cost” of needing to reduce all qualities down to a single metric.

How is the AbundX different from distributed currencies like BitCoin?

BitCoin is similar to AbundX in the lack of a central authority that can track and control supply. But BitCoin derives its value through scarcity and thus encourages hoarding. Like central currencies, BitCoin require all qualities to be expressed in a single metric.

How is the Abundant Exchange different from 1:1 trading networks?

One-to-one trading networks (traditional definition of barter) are nearly all operated by a single entity. You must create an account with that entity and typically have to pay a fee for each transaction or a membership fee. Because the AbundX allows exchanges between multiple people, it is much more likely to create a circle of offers and needs than a one-to-one exchange.

How does the AbundX Agent protect my privacy?

When you joint the exchange you select an alias. Everything you do in the exchange is associated with this alias. There is no central controller that can link your alias with your real name. You can have authorities validate claims you make without providing your real name or any other information about yourself that you do not want to share with the entire exchange. For more information of how Verified Anonymity works, see QualityofID.com.

Don’t I reveal personal information to the exchange as soon as I give or receive something?

If you decide to give or receive something that requires exchanging personal information (like your address), you create a Connection with the other person that is independent of the exchange. You decide to whom you will give your information. You may be willing to give your address to someone who has a long history with the exchange and has been validated as a real person by a respected law firm, but not to someone new to the exchange with no validating authorities to vouch for them.

Does it matter where my Agent is hosted?

There is no required relationship between the location where the AbundX Agent is hosted and the location where a particular Abundant Exchange is centered.

Can I use a AbundX Agent for a distributed social network?

Yes, Agents support secure, authenticated peer-to-peer communications and controlled access to information to individuals or groups (Graphs).

Why can’t I use a p2p personal data service (PDS) like the Locker Project, Friendica, or Diaspora in the Abundant Exchange?
  1. Personal data services focus on personal data visibility and tracking. Agents focus on quality of information (see QualityofID.com) where identity, control, and security follow.
  2. PDS’s use existing Internet standards for security, which gives the attacker the advantage over the defender.
  3. Connections between Agents are mutual, meaning either party can sever the Connection preventing either Agent accessing information about the other the other.
  4. The Agent implementation allows Agents to Connect to Graphs instead of requiring everyone to connect to everyone else in order to exchange information.
  5. Agents are able to provide proofs of conformance to key behaviors like information access, and the distributed n-way transaction that makes the AbundX possible.

Distributed economy requirements

  • Peer-to-Peer: all participants are on an equal footing and none has special functionality or privileged information that could be turned into an advantage over others—there are no servers, administrators, or directories
  • Transactional: the agreements to exchange goods and services either all occur or none occur, and a unit of goods or services is not provided or received more than once—in other words, cannot participate in multiple transactions
  • N-way exchange: the exchange can link any number of “offers” and “needs” to create a valid transaction (balanced state)
  • Non-contractual: the obligation to provide an offer is independent of the fulfilling of a need—agreements are enforced by reputation, not legal constraints
  • Privacy: Personally identifying information (PII) should not be required to participate in the exchange. PII provided in the exchange of goods or services should be limited to the parties needing that information to complete delivery
  • Stored value: in the form of promises—as confidence, capabilities, and reputation grows, then higher value goods and services will be exchanged over longer time frames
  • Subjective: value placed on goods or services should be able to vary based upon quality, circumstance, constraints, and any quantifiable and non-quantifiable relevant variables based upon the perceived value of the participants
  • Control of source and destination of offers: the ability to not only choose who you wish to receive goods and services from, but also who you will receive your goods your services
  • Reputation system: assurances that goods and services will be delivered as expected relative to the value of goods or services
  • Verifiable: a mechanism for providing evidence of claims, such as promises, references, recommendations, certifications, and goods or services delivery
  • Transparent: all transactions are visible to all participants
  • Flexible: there are no limits or standards imposed on criteria or qualities of goods and services exchanged except that emerge by local consensus
  • Distributed semantic resolution: there must be a method for participants to match offers with needs, but without a central authority or standard dictating semantics â€“ the mechanism for resolution of semantics need only support peer-to-peer, in other words, only two entities need to agree upon definition of terms used in an exchange, not everyone in the exchange
  • Currency interface: ability to trade currency for goods and services or promise of goods or services and to extract currency from the exchange in return for goods or services or promise of goods or services. This capability is supported the same as any other delivery of goods, but we are working on a more flexible implementation.

Other economies

The problem with currency

Note: There are many much better descriptions of the problems with currency-based economies. Among them we recommend Eisenstein’s Sacred Economics and Rushkoff’s Life Inc. Here we attempt to present an abbreviated summary.

A centralized currency creates artificial scarcity, which in turn gives the advantage to those with more of the central currency over those with less. The need to convert all value (goods or services) to the centralized currency allows for centralized control of the currency equivalent (financial instrument), which in turn results in inflated prices and more artificial scarcity.

The dependency on centralized currencies inhibits the exchange of goods and services where the currency is in short supply. Financial instruments create artificial scarcity and imbalances in the exchange of value as the trade of financial instruments captures more currency.

The bias of centralized currencies is to centralize. The rich get richer and locations with more currency accumulate it while locations that generate less currency will be able to retain less and less currency. The problems of Spain and Greece can be seen as the result of the Euro accumulating in wealthier nations and lack of currency inhibiting production and exchange of goods and services in poorer countries. The current problems in the US can be traced back to deregulation of banks in the 1980’s allowing national banks. The result was that savings were not invested locally, but in vehicles that made more money for the financial institution, and interest payments and banking fees are not put back into the local economy, but instead accumulated in banking centers like New York City.

Nor does the problem with centralized currencies stop at national borders. National currencies are traded against currencies of wealthier nations. Debt in currencies from wealthier nations creates a flow of value to the wealthier nation in the form of debt and foreign ownership. When the debt cannot be met, then the World Bank steps in and lends the country more money in return for concessions that result in value leaving the country at a faster pace. That value may be natural resources, or labor to produce goods and services at lower rates than in wealthier nations. The result is higher prices for more goods produced locally and lower wages to buy them.

A more subtle, but much more important problem with currency is requirement to reduce all value to a single metric. This discussion is beyond the scope of this presentation and has been well articulated by Charles Eisenstein in Sacred Economics: Money Gift, and Society in the Age of Transition, July 2011.

Non-Currency Exchanges

Creating centralized local exchanges may help with currency scarcity, but they are limited to facilitating exchange of like services (for example a baby sitting cooperative), or a one-to-one trading system (for example, farm work for tomatoes). Matching offers and needs between two people to make an exchange acceptable to both does not happen often enough to make this a viable alternative economy.

Non-Government Backed Currencies

Barter exchanges using an exchange currency, resource-based currencies, and locally issued currencies can be considered “non-government backed currency” for the purposes of this summary.

The only manageable way to match needs with offers in a centralized exchange is to convert each good or service to some common value or local currency. This new non-government currency is still centralized and has all the liabilities of a national currency, only at a smaller scale. Even if it is based upon some valuable resource, like power or food, the tendency to tie the exchange, resource, or local currency to a wealthier economy will eventually lead to a steady flow of value out of the local economy or exchange network. The tendency to link non-government currency to the government currency minimizes any advantage the local currency brings while creating additional liabilities, such as instability, inflation, and debt in the larger currency.

Apart from the liabilities of a centralized currency, subjective value would be very hard to support in a centralized exchange. For example, Alice might be willing to work one hour a month from now for one and a half bushels of tomatoes today. Or Bill may be willing to pay two bushels for an hour of plumbing work from Alice, but only 1 bushel for an hour of plumbing from Paul. The semantics of the central exchange would have to be identified, standardized, implemented, thoroughly tested and understood by all participants before they could be useful.

Trust

Centralized local exchanges must also deal with the issue of trust. Tom, Alice, and Bill must all trust the central exchange to execute accurately and fairly. They must further trust the exchange to validate each participant. This leads to standardizing on the lowest common denominator rather than rewarding excellence.

Participating in an AbundX

Quick answers for how to use the AbundX Agent and new AbundX Exchanges

Note: See the detailed description below for procedures to create your own AbundX Agent and new Abundant Exchanges.

How do I let others know I have something to offer?

Create a content entry of the item or service along with descriptive and qualifying labels and drag it to the AbundX Connection.

How do I find something I need in the AbundX?

If you need something specific, then you navigate the labels created by others until you find something you would like in exchange for what you are offering.

What if I can’t find what I’m looking for?

You create a “need” such as “yard work wanted”. The more labels you assign to your need, the more likely someone will discover it.

How do I come to an agreement with someone about getting or providing a good or service?

The person making an offer can modify the offer or make a new offer to meet a specific need. For example, I have a used dining room set that I would like to get something for, but I am not sure what. I see that someone is offering yard work and I think 3 hours of yard work would be a fair exchange for the furniture. The more flexible I am with what I can get in exchange, then the quicker my transaction will execute.

How do I know when my offer and need has been finalized in an AbundX transaction?

You will get notification that one of your offers has been conditionally accepted and one of your needs conditionally met. When everyone in the circle of exchanges has confirmed the conditional acceptance, then the transaction will execute and each offer becomes an obligation and each need is waiting for fulfillment.

More detail on how the Abundant Exchange will work

How do I set up an AbundX Agent?

By installing one yourself or using a Hosting Provider. All AbundX Agents will have al least one existing Connection, which can list available Abundant Exchanges and facilitate Connecting your AbundX Agent to them. The AbundX will then appear as a Connection in your AbundX Agent.

How do I create an Abundant Exchange?

Creating an exchange is similar to defining a new address where everyone will post messages. Like putting notes on a community bulletin board. AbundX.com will help coordinate establishment of Abundant Exchanges and provide a directory service.

How do I join an AbundX Exchange?

People join the exchange by being introduced to the exchange by someone already connected to it. In other words, anyone can use the bulletin board who has been told where it is located.

AbundX.com can help bootstrap exchanges by providing directory serices and Introductions to new members, but Abundant Exchanges can be established without AbundX.com involvement.

People join the exchange under an alias. This protects everyone’s privacy. Everyone can see every message posted by everyone else, but cannot know the identity of the person. People in the exchange can connect directly to each other to exchange information outside of the exchange, if they wish. For example, if I wish to have someone come to my house to pick up my couch.

How do I post an offer or need?

Making an offer or expressing a need requires creating a message and assigning it to the exchange.

For example, I have a couch I would like to exchange something for. I would create a message designated as an “offer” and include one or more pictures and a description.

How do I find offers and needs?

Members find messages by filtering based upon labels assigned to the posts. In our example, I would put various labels on my couch, such as “couch”, “furniture”, “gently used”, and so on. There is no predefined set of labels that people must use.

How are offers and needs matched?

A participant can link any number of offers with any number of needs. When a circle of offers is identified by the Agents involved, then each participant is notified that their offer has been conditionally accepted and a corresponding need met. When all participants have agreed, then the transaction executes and all the offers and needs involved change status to “awaiting fulfillment.” When the offer is eventually delivered, then the AbundX Agent’s owner mush acknowledge receipt by changing the need status to “complete”. Any additional information can be added to the fulfilled need to contribute to the reputation system (see below).

How are offers fulfilled?

If fulfilling and offer or receiving one requires personal information to be exchanged, like name and address, then the parties involved in the giving and receiving must create a direct connection between their Agents. This is accomplished by both parties requesting and then accepting an Introduction between their two Agents. Once connected, each Agent can communicate with the other without the information being accessible to the exchange.

What is the Reputation System and how does it work?

Anyone can provide verification of claims to the exchange. For example, I wish people in the exchange to know that I have lived in Littletown for 20 years. I could arrange with my lawyer to validate (through his Agent) that information with anyone in the exchange under the alias I am using. Someone who wants some indication I am trust worthy can present my alias and claims to my lawyer (with or without using an Agent), who will validate my claims after confirming with me it is ok to do so. This can all be done automatically using Agents

I can also agree with other exchange members to recommend me by acting as a verifier of claims my Agent makes (as in a peer-to-peer web of trust), by posting a testimonial message, or by reviewing a transaction favorably. Anyone in the exchange can access all messages associated with me and thus draw their own conclusions regarding my trustworthiness.

Thus, validation of claims by authorities independent of the exchange, peer review, and history in the exchange create a reputation system that is extremely difficult to game. Visualization and automation of this reputation system is a major portion of the effort we hope to fund from the Kickstarter campaign.

For more information on the reputation system see QualityofID.com/Web_of_Proveneance.
